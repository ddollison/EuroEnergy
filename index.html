Patterns of Energy Production and Consumption within Europe and within the Republic of Ireland

A project by Drew Dollison, Jayden Rogers, and Gretchen Klock

.

Goals and Objectives

We aim to visualize patterns of energy production, transmission, and consumption in Ireland. This includes studying issues of access to electricity (including affordability) and the security of electricity. Objective: we plan to study changes in the energy grid in Ireland over time. Thus, we will study how the country of Ireland worked over time to urbanize and electrify. In doing so, we will be able to identify possible historic and present day equity issues (such as inequality in energy access and negative externalities associated with electricity production and transmission). Through mapping energy sources, we will also be able to visualize the balance of domestic energy sources (peat coal, natural gas, and hydroelectric, later wind) and imported energy sources (oil, natural gas, coal). Additionally, to best understand energy energy consumption, we plan to study the spatial relationship between population and energy use. This will also allow us to compare energy consumption between urban and rural areas to understand the relationship between urbanization and energy use.
